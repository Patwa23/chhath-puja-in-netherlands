Traditional Overview:

Day 1

Nahay Khay

Sunday, October 26

The first day involves ritual bathing in a holy river/pond and consuming a single, pure, vegetarian meal, marking the start of the vow.

Day 2

Kharna (Lohanda)

Monday, October 27

A strict fast without water is observed until sunset, broken by a special offering. This evening also hosts the **Sandhya Arghya** at the venue.

Day 3 - Vrat Continues

Sandhya Arghya

Monday, October 27 (Evening)

The main offering is made to the **setting Sun God** at the riverbank/pool. The 36-hour fast begins after *Kharna* (Day 2).

Day 4 - CONCLUSION

Usha Arghya (Parana)

Tuesday, October 28

Devotees offer prayers and *Arghya* to the **rising Sun God** (Usha) and break their long, arduous fast afterward (*Parana*).

Worship of Surya Dev & Chhathi Maiya

The festival is dedicated to the Sun God, Surya, who is the source of life force, and his sister, Chhathi Maiya (Goddess Shashthi), who is revered as the protector of children and the giver of offspring.

üïäÔ∏è

Purity and Gratitude

The rigorous 36-hour fast (*Nirjala Vrat*) symbolizes self-discipline and purification of the body and soul. It is a profound expression of gratitude toward nature's life-sustaining elements (Sun and Water).

üìö

Mythological Roots

Legends trace the festival back to the Vedic period. It is mentioned in the Mahabharata, where Draupadi and the Pandavas performed the Puja, and is also linked to Karna, the son of Surya.
